## Purpose

This is the **formal GLBA §501(b) Risk Assessment Report** — the statutory deliverable of Phase 03 and the document presented to the **Board of Directors / Audit Committee**. It satisfies the Interagency Guidelines Establishing Information Security Standards requirement that the Bank **identify reasonably foreseeable internal and external threats to customer NPI, assess the likelihood and potential damage, and assess the sufficiency of policies and controls**. It consolidates the analyses in 03.01–03.09 into an executive-level record with a conclusion, recommendations, and a Board sign-off block.

## Executive Summary

Cornerstone Community Bank conducted a comprehensive information-security risk assessment of the threats to customer NPI and to the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of Bank systems. The assessment followed NIST SP 800-30 methodology and the FFIEC Information Security and Cybersecurity guidance, using the sunset FFIEC CAT structure mapped forward to NIST CSF 2.0.

The assessment identified **42 risks**, rated **8 High, 18 Moderate, and 16 Low**. The Bank's **overall inherent risk profile is Moderate**, with **External Threats elevated to Significant**. NPI resides across **22 systems** within a 140-system enterprise inventory; core banking and digital banking are **outsourced to Meridian Core Services, LLC** under SOC 1 Type II / SOC 2 Type II assurance. No condition was identified that, on its own, elevates the enterprise profile above Moderate. The eight High risks are outside the Board's risk appetite and are assigned mandatory treatment plans that drive the Phase 04 control program and the Phase 05 maturity remediation.

## Methodology

The assessment was conducted per the Phase 03 methodology (03.01) and produced ratings using the 5×5 scoring model (03.06).

```mermaid
flowchart LR
    S["Scope &amp; assets — Phase 02"] --> T["Threat analysis — 03.02"]
    T --> N["NPI impact — 03.03"]
    N --> V["Vulnerability assessment — 03.04"]
    V --> IRP["Inherent risk profile — 03.05"]
    IRP --> SC["Scoring 5x5 — 03.06"]
    SC --> REG["Risk register (42) — 03.07"]
    REG --> TR["Treatment &amp; appetite — 03.08"]
    TR --> GAP["Preliminary control gaps — 03.09"]
    GAP --> RPT["This report — Board §501(b)"]
```

The methodology assessed likelihood (threat frequency, capability, and existing control strength) and impact (weighted explicitly by NPI sensitivity and volume), scoring each risk on anchored 1–5 scales and mapping the product through fixed High/Moderate/Low thresholds.

## Key Findings

1. **Overall inherent risk is Moderate**, appropriate for a ~$1.2B single-state community bank with an outsourced core and a standard channel/product mix.
2. **External Threats are Significant** — persistent phishing, ransomware, account-takeover, and BEC pressure against a retail-facing bank with 62,000 digital users.
3. **Eight High risks are outside appetite** and concentrate in NPI confidentiality, access/MFA, fraud, ransomware/recovery, external patching, insider misuse, and critical-provider concentration.
4. **Control gaps cluster in Protect**, with elevated Detect/Respond/Recover needs — a preliminary view that feeds the Phase 05 maturity assessment (28 gaps vs an Intermediate target).
5. **Third-party concentration on Meridian** is a material dependency, managed through SOC reliance, enhanced oversight, and contractual/insurance transfer.
6. **No material weakness in the risk-management process itself** was identified; the register, scoring, and governance are sound and examinable.

## Conclusions

The Bank has a complete, evidence-based understanding of the reasonably foreseeable threats to customer NPI and of the sufficiency of its current safeguards. The **overall Moderate inherent risk** is consistent with the institution's size, complexity, and profile. The **eight High risks** are known, owned, and slated for treatment; none is being accepted as-is. Existing controls are partially effective and, once matured through the Phase 04 program and Phase 05 remediation, are expected to bring residual risk to the target **Low-to-Moderate, well-managed** posture. The risk-management process itself is sound and provides a reliable basis for Board oversight under GLBA §501(b).
